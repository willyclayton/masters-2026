Smart Combos

Correlated Parlays

Same-player multi-leg parlays where outcomes are correlated — if one hits, the others are much more likely. Books underprice these.

Why correlated parlays have edge: DK prices each leg independently, but if Schauffele is having a great week (makes cut), he's much more likely to also finish top 20 and top 10. The conditional probability is higher than the implied parlay odds suggest.

Feature: Correlated Parlays — Exploits a known sportsbook weakness: DK prices parlay legs independently, but some outcomes are highly correlated. A "Same-Player Ladder" (make cut + top 20 + top 10) has much higher conditional probability than the parlay odds imply. Also pairs finish positions with nationality props (if Aberg finishes top 20, he's almost certainly Top European). Shows correlation percentage and true conditional probability.

The Amen Corner Survivor
Pattern: The eventual winner plays Amen Corner (11, 12, 13) at even par or better in the final round 80% of the time. Surviving Amen Corner on Sunday is the single best predictor of who wins.

2026 implication: Players with the best historical Amen Corner scoring (Scheffler -0.38 avg, McIlroy -0.22 avg) have a structural edge.
Pattern #3 — 65% historical hit rate
The Experience Tax
Pattern: First-timers at Augusta make the cut only 38% of the time vs. 62% for players with 3+ appearances. The course punishes inexperience relentlessly.

2026 implication: Fade first-timers in make-cut markets. Target experienced players like Lowry (8 appearances), Fleetwood (7), Rose (21) whose make-cut odds may be too generous.
